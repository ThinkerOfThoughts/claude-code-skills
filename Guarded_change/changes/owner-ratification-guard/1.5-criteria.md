# 1.5 — Criteria: the owner-ratification guard

Acceptance bar for the skill-file edits. Each criterion is labeled **gating** (must be verified
by execution at stage 8 for the change to be accepted) or **advisory**. Verification is against
the *edited skill source*, and — for the behavioral criteria — by running a **cold reviewer**
under the new charter against the incident artifacts (the representativeness check: the guard is
proven by catching the real incident, per the spike-representativeness lesson).

---

## Behavioral criteria (does the guard actually catch the incident — *because of the guard*?)

**Causal-toggle discipline (applies to C1, C2, C14) — the spike-representativeness lesson.**
Each behavioral catch is verified under **both** the pre-edit and the post-edit charter/text on
the *same* input. A criterion **PASSES only if the post-edit version raises the finding AND the
pre-edit version does NOT raise it, or raises it materially weaker.** If the pre-edit version
already raises it at full strength, the "catch" is the base model, not the guard — the criterion
is **not** satisfied (the "verifies criteria not intent" trap; a strong base model flags a
self-announcing incident regardless). The toggle is the gating pass-condition, not an
instrumentation footnote. If the incident proves too self-announcing to show a clean toggle, the
catch is re-established on a **constructed, less-obvious** case that the pre-edit lens misses.

**C1 — Catches FM1 (non-disambiguating answer recorded as a ruling), because of RAT1. GATING.**
A cold subagent, given the incident's `{1-spec.md (the "OWNER RULING option c" section),
3-review.md (the "OWNER MUST RATIFY" finding)}` **plus the owner's verbatim exchange** (the
option presentation + the cost-only answer), must, **under the post-edit charter**, flag that
"OWNER RULING: option c" is **not** a valid ratification (the answer addresses cost only; it does
not disambiguate (b) from (c)) and call for a **re-ask**; and, **under the pre-edit charter**,
must NOT raise that finding at full strength (causal toggle). *Pass:* post-edit flags (≥ major)
citing the non-disambiguating answer; pre-edit does not (or materially weaker). *Verified by
execution* (both runs recorded in `8-conformance.md`).

**C2 — Catches FM2 (elaboration inflation), because of RAT2. GATING.**
The same regime on the ratified phrase + its elaboration (`1-spec.md:131-144`): **post-edit**
(CH12/RAT2) flags that the elaboration adds operative commitments ("harness owns ONLY loop
mechanics / AgentBob owns ONLY his words / harness runs the cycle") **not entailed by** the
ratified phrase, naming ≥1 inflated term; **pre-edit** does not raise it at full strength.
*Verified by execution* (both runs).

**C3 — Precision: does NOT false-flag a genuinely-compliant ratification or a legitimate
elaboration. GATING.**
Two **constructed** fixtures (built in the change folder, ground truth known):
  (a) a **compliant ratification record** that actually carries the RAT1 fields — verbatim
      options + verbatim owner response that *does* disambiguate the flagged axis + the mapping +
      a durable-source citation — under the post-edit charter must **NOT** be flagged as invalid;
  (b) a **legitimate elaboration** whose operative terms are genuinely entailed by the ratified
      phrase must **NOT** be flagged as inflation (RAT2 precision — covers CG-3).
*Pass:* no RAT1/RAT2 finding against either fixture. Guards against the guard becoming a
false-positive generator (CP8 precision). *Note:* the incident's own voice-contamination ruling
(`3-review.md:39`) is **not** used as the clean specimen — it is a *paraphrased* ruling that
itself lacks the verbatim-artifact fields, so under the new standard it too is under-recorded (a
motivating example, not a counterexample). *Verified by execution.*

**C3p — Catches a fabricated/reconstructed "verbatim" (provenance sub-mode), because of RAT1's
provenance requirement. GATING.**
A constructed record whose "owner's verbatim words" are a **reconstruction with no durable-source
citation** (the compaction sub-mode the spec advertises as re-confirmable) must, under the
post-edit charter, be flagged as **not spot-checkable** (missing provenance anchor) — with the
pre-edit charter not raising it (causal toggle). *Pass:* post-edit flags the missing provenance;
pre-edit does not. This verifies the advertised "re-confirmable across compaction" capability
rather than shipping it as an untested claim (CG-1). *Verified by execution.*

**C4 — A clean RAT1/RAT2 verdict must be earned. GATING.**
The new charter text must make a clean fidelity verdict on a finding carrying a recorded
owner-ruling **valid only if** the review shows the ratification-record audit was actually done
(the presented options + owner's verbatim words cited, the mapping confirmed, the elaboration's
operative terms traced) — mirroring the existing "clean factual/fidelity lens must be earned"
guard. *Verified:* the earned-clean requirement is present in `charter.md` and `stage-3.md`
(CH11/CH12) and is checkable by a positive per-site assertion (see C7).

## Structural / consistency criteria (skill-file integrity)

**C5 — Canonical statement + consistent cross-references. GATING.**
Each guard (RAT1, RAT2 canonical in `stage-3.md`; RAT3 canonical in `METHODOLOGY.md`
"Human-in-the-loop") has **exactly one canonical full statement**; every other site
(`charter.md` fidelity lens, `stage-1.md` recording contract, `stage-4.md` gate, `SKILL.md`,
and the `METHODOLOGY.md`/`SKILL.md` cross-pointers) states its operative slice and
**references** the canonical home, with no contradiction between sites. *Verified:* per-site
positive assertion — each expected site contains the expected reference, and a read-through
confirms no site states a rule that contradicts the canonical text.

**C6 — Gate wiring is complete end-to-end. GATING.**
The edited files together implement the full path: escalation (charter fidelity lens) →
author records a **ratification record** (stage-1) → cold audit CH11/CH12 (stage-3) → gate
routes resolved-only-if-audit-passes, else re-ask (stage-4) → HIL stop condition (SKILL.md +
METHODOLOGY.md) → **under delegation, the stop bubbles to the actual human, not the orchestrator
(RAT3)**. No link is missing (the "verifies criteria not intent" trap: the end-to-end goal is
the criterion, not just one enabling primitive). *Verified:* trace each link to its site.

**C7 — Positive per-site assertion oracle exists and can fail. GATING.**
Conformance for C4/C5/C6 uses a **positive per-site assertion** (each expected marker/phrase is
present at its expected file) rather than a bare absence sweep, and the oracle is demonstrated
able to fail (run it against the **pre-edit** tree and confirm it reports the markers missing).
*Verified by execution* at stage 8 (H6).

**C8 — live copy == source copy. GATING.**
After the edit, `diff -r ~/.claude/skills/guarded-change/ <source>` shows the installed skill
byte-identical to the repo source on all shipped files (`SKILL.md`, `METHODOLOGY.md`,
`stages/*`). *Verified by execution* (the `diff`).

**C9 — Behavior-preservation for the position-sensitive assembly. GATING.**
The skill files are a position-sensitive assembly (prompts). Every existing rule/lens that was
**not** the target of this change still reads and functions as before — in particular the five
lenses, the existing discipline bullets, CH8/CH9/CH10, and the iteration-cap re-ask machinery
are unchanged in meaning; new material is **added** where it governs the right actor at the
right stage, and does not displace an element that worked because of its position (e.g. the
fidelity lens's existing "untrusted until owner confirms" clause remains the lead, with the
ratification clause built onto it, not replacing it). *Verified:* a diff-scoped read confirming
only additive/extending edits to the named anchors, no unrelated rule altered.

**C10 — No project specifics leak into Layer 1. GATING.**
The added text names no companion-emergence specifics (no "Bob", "AgentBob", "T4", "monologue",
persona names). The incident is referenced only in the change-folder docs, never in the shipped
skill files. *Verified:* grep the shipped files for the specific terms → none present.

**C13 — RAT3 stated + consistent. GATING (structural).**
RAT3 has a canonical full statement in `METHODOLOGY.md` "Human-in-the-loop" and an operative
pointer in `SKILL.md` "Stop-for-human"; both name the two obligations — the subagent **halts and
returns the question verbatim marked for relay** (does not self-resolve/proceed) and the
orchestrator **relays to the human and never answers as the owner** — with no contradiction.
*Verified:* positive per-site assertion (each site contains its RAT3 marker).

**C14 — RAT3 behavioral (subagent half only), by execution. GATING.**
A cold subagent, told to run the loop under the **new** SKILL/METHODOLOGY text and handed the
incident's FM1 stop-for-human situation, **halts and returns the question marked for verbatim
relay to the human** and does **NOT** self-resolve it into the recommended option or proceed.
*Causal toggle (per the discipline above):* PASS requires the **pre-edit** runner on the same
situation to behave materially worse — self-resolve, proceed, or omit the "relay verbatim, don't
answer" marking. **Scope honesty:** C14 tests only the **subagent half** of RAT3 (halt + emit the
marked human-gate packet), which is skill-enforceable. RAT3's **orchestrator half** ("relay to
the human, never answer as the owner") governs an actor **outside** the skill and is **not**
gated here — it is a caller-side operating instruction (see C15). If the pre-edit text already
halts (LA-3 — the skill already has stop-for-human machinery), the honest result is "the RAT3
subagent-half is a *clarifying/marking* addition, not a behavior change" — recorded as such, not
forced to a false toggle. *Verified by execution.*

**C15 — RAT3 orchestrator-half is correctly scoped as a caller instruction, not claimed as
skill-enforced. GATING (structural).**
The shipped text must **not** claim the orchestrator-half is structurally enforced by the loop.
It states plainly that the subagent-half is skill-enforceable while the orchestrator-half ("never
answer as the owner") is a **caller-side** obligation (living in the caller's operating
instructions / CLAUDE.md), reinforced — not guaranteed — by RAT1's verbatim-provenance
spot-check. *Verified:* positive assertion that E10/E11 carry the scoping caveat and do not
assert full structural closure.
